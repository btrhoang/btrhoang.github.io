A Proud Mom
folder [MUN]  

I arrived home at 10 p.m. on the final day of the three-day conference. I didn’t cry, neither in the closing ceremony nor the dinner party, despite seeing many of my members failing to choke back their tears. Instead, I calmly slept for twelve hours to replenish my energy, getting myself ready for the upcoming post-event tasks.

As the Deputy Secretary General, I long attached myself to the responsibility of remaining mentally strong on all occasions so that my members can always have a firm shoulder to lean on. Many a time I saw Bennard and I in our depths of despair when faced with apparently insoluble challenges. Still, we had to keep most things to ourselves while motivating everyone to keep going with their assigned tasks. We prioritized others’ mental health and emotions, so sometimes neglected our own. We still kept that habit, even though Bennard had struck the gavel, signaling the end of NKO MUN’ 23.

Now, writing this appreciation post, I allowed myself to return to my more girly version, the more sentimental one. I remembered many, many delegates congratulated me on such a well thought out event, and others saying “Impressive organization!” when I gave them our “Thank you letter” while signing placards. I also vividly recalled the way everyone sang along with our performers and the homely atmosphere that we managed to create in the whole conference, especially in the closing ceremony.

I felt like a proud mom, although not a perfect one. I usually got mad at my members when things went wrong, and apologized to them 30 minutes later. I was sometimes not far-sighted or detail-oriented enough to make our event flawless or exceptional. But like other moms, I’m sure I have done everything imaginable to make this event possible, and to ensure that every single delegate, chair, and organizer felt at home.

This is not the first organization I have worked at as a leader, but the only one that has presented me with unprecedented obstacles that challenged me to my limits. Bennard and I have grown a whole lot after those hardships, and we hope to have created a space for all of you to develop and explore yourselves. And it’s touching for me to see us becoming increasingly close together after those difficulties.

I love every single one of you guys. I remember every detail of the memories each of you shares with me. I can’t be so sure whether we will have a chance to reunite, but it’s a pleasure for me to have met, talked to, and hugged you.

Thank you all for being an indispensable part of Nang Khieu Open Model United Nations - NKO MUN, and an indispensable part of me.
